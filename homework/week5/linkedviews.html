Where in Europe is life most pleasant?

The quality of life in Europe differs per country. The NUMBEO database provides data about health care, pollution, cost of living etc. from countries worldwide. In the map below countries are colored based on the Quality of Life Index (QoLI) of NUMBEO. For the coloring, a range from yellow (low QoLI) to green (high QoLI) is used. The aim of this 'mini-research' was to determine whether we would see the same trend in quality of life in other databases for these countries. The OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development) is an organisation which also provides data about health, economics, social and environmental factors. From the Better Life Index of the OECD database five indicators were selected, namely: 'Employment rate', 'Quality of support network', 'Educational attainment', 'Water quality' and 'Self-reported health'. By clicking on a country on the map, a bar chart will appear which shows how well that country scores for each of the five indicators. Both datasets show the same trend. Countries which have a high Quality of Life Index also have a higher percentage of employment rate, quality of support network, educational attainment, self-reported health and a better water quality. From these data life in Switzerland seems the most pleasant... :)
